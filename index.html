Our Core Services

NOWA combines finance and mobility to create a truly connected community experience.

The NOWA Digital Credits Ecosystem

A stable, digital credit system designed for local trade within Nakuru, ensuring money stays within the community to fuel local businesses and opportunities.

For Users

  • Accessible: Easily top-up credits at local agents.
  • Safe: Pay instantly and securely using a simple mobile app.
  • Incentivized: Earn rewards for local purchases.

For Merchants

  • Zero Hidden Fees: Low, transparent transaction costs.
  • Instant Settlement: Payments credited immediately; no waiting time.
  • Wider Customer Base: Access to the growing NOWA user community.

The Seamless Transport Platform

A cashless, organized system for booking and paying for local transport services (matatus, taxis, and boda-bodas), making mobility safer and more efficient.

Safety & Convenience

  • Safety First: Vetted drivers and GPS tracking for all journeys.
  • Fair Fares: Transparent, standardized pricing; no cash haggling.
  • Convenience: Book and pay directly using NOWA digital credits.

Driver Support

  • Stability: Digital tools for organized route management.
  • Training: Resources for professional development and service standards.
  • Earning: Predictable, stable income through cashless payments.

Our Commitment to Nakuru

Mission & Vision

Mission: To empower the Nakuru community by providing an inclusive, stable, and accessible digital ecosystem for local commerce and safe, efficient mobility.

Vision: A financially resilient and digitally connected Nakuru where opportunity, economic stability, and safe transport are accessible to every resident.

Our Sustainability Model

NOWA is built to be self-sustaining, ensuring long-term community benefits through transparent and minimal fees, avoiding reliance on endless external funding.

  • Micro-Transaction Fees (Primary): A very small, transparent fee applied to merchant transactions—lower than traditional providers.
  • Premium Merchant Tools: Optional, value-added services like advanced analytics and loyalty program management for merchants.
  • Strategic Partnerships: Collaborations with institutions and NGOs focused on economic inclusion and transport safety.

Our Journey Ahead

Q1: Pilot & Launch

Digital Credits Public Launch

Complete beta testing, launch the user and merchant apps, and onboard the first 100 key merchants in the Nakuru CBD area.

Q2: Growth & Expansion

Transport Platform Rollout

Digital Transformation in Local Economies

Published on January 15, 2025 • By Aisha K. • 5 min read

Digital credits are revolutionizing the way local economies function, particularly in communities like Nakuru. By keeping money circulating within the local ecosystem, NOWA's digital credits system ensures that economic benefits stay where they matter most.

This approach not only strengthens local businesses but also fosters a sense of community ownership and responsibility. Merchants report increased customer loyalty and reduced cash handling costs, while users enjoy greater convenience and security in their daily transactions.

Key Benefits for Nakuru

  • Increased local spending by 35%
  • Reduced transaction costs for merchants
  • Improved financial inclusion for underserved communities
  • Enhanced economic resilience during market fluctuations

As we look to the future, digital transformation in local economies represents a powerful tool for inclusive growth and sustainable development. NOWA is proud to be at the forefront of this revolution in Kenya.

Building Trust in Fintech

Published on January 10, 2025 • By David O. • 4 min read

Trust is the foundation of any financial technology ecosystem. At NOWA, we believe that community-driven approaches are essential for building lasting trust in fintech solutions.

By involving local leaders, merchants, and residents in the development and implementation process, we ensure that our solutions are not only technologically sound but also culturally appropriate and community-focused.

Our Trust-Building Strategy

  1. Community Engagement: Regular town hall meetings and feedback sessions
  2. Transparent Operations: Open-source components and public audits
  3. Local Partnerships: Collaboration with trusted community institutions
  4. Education Programs: Free workshops on digital literacy and security

This collaborative approach has led to higher adoption rates and greater user satisfaction, proving that when communities are at the center of fintech innovation, everyone wins. With 95% user satisfaction rates, NOWA is setting the standard for trustworthy fintech in Kenya.
